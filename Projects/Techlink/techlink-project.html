Delivery Risk & Client Retention Analysis (Excel and Power BI)

Overview

TechLink’s rapid growth is now straining delivery execution and threatening future revenue. Although high‑level delivery indicators remain positive, closer review shows clear pressure points: more projects nearing deadline risk, delivery delays concentrated in specific services and regions, and declining repeat client engagement. These issues are already weakening delivery reliability and client retention in measurable ways.

This analysis examines project‑level delivery data to pinpoint where execution risk is emerging, how backlog pressure is building, and how sustained delays reduce client re‑engagement. By tracking how work progresses over time and where pressure accumulates, it connects delivery performance directly to client continuity and revenue risk.

Business Context

TechLink Consulting delivers project‑based technology services across:

  • Infrastructure & Cloud
  • Software Development
  • Data & Analytics
  • Security & Compliance
  • IT Support

The Problem

Leadership relied on completed outcomes, such as on‑time delivery rates, which obscured risks emerging within active projects. As a result, delivery issues were often identified too late to prevent escalation.

  • Limited visibility into delivery risk
    Delays were concentrated in specific services and regions, but existing reporting failed to surface these hotspots early enough to intervene.
  • Project status masked execution pressure
    “In Progress” status provided little insight into whether work was truly on track or quietly slipping behind.
  • Retention risk was difficult to anticipate
    Client satisfaction was measured, but its connection to delivery performance and repeat engagement was unclear.
  • Intervention was reactive rather than proactive
    Without a unified view of delivery risk, service performance, and revenue exposure, action often occurred only after delays had already escalated.

The result was a decision gap that increased operational risk and threatened long‑term client value.

Analytical Approach

The analysis was designed to move beyond static outcome reporting and focus on active execution behavior, where risk first appears. Key steps included:

  • Structuring the dataset at the project level to track full lifecycle performance
  • Reconstructing client engagement history to accurately capture repeat behavior
  • Developing time‑based risk indicators such as project age and deadline proximity
  • Evaluating performance through both historical outcomes and active risk signals
  • Segmenting results by service line, region, and client to identify concentration of risk

The final output was an interactive Power BI dashboard supported by structured Excel analysis, enabling leadership to identify pressure early and act decisively.

Data Preparation & Feature Engineering

Data Preparation in Excel

Data preparation was conducted in Excel to clean, validate, and structure the dataset for reliable analysis.

  • Standardized date fields to ensure valid project timelines and accurate time‑based analysis
  • Cleaned and normalized categorical fields for consistency
  • Identified and resolved missing values, outliers, and anomalies
  • Applied data validation rules to detect incomplete or invalid records
  • Built logical checks using IF() and IF(AND()) to flag incorrect timelines
  • Removed duplicate records using COUNTIF() and conditional checks
  • Cross‑validated totals and aggregates using SUMIF()
  • Aligned related data across tables using XLOOKUP()
  • Structured analysis‑ready tables for Power BI integration

Feature Engineering

  • Active Project Age – elapsed time for ongoing work
  • Deadline Urgency Signals – combined time elapsed with time remaining
  • Aging Buckets – 0–90, 91–180, 181–270, 270+ days
  • Rule‑Based Delivery Stages – replaced unreliable status labels with: On Track, Due Soon, Overdue, Completed On Time/Late
  • Delivery Variance – quantified delays relative to planned timelines
  • Repeat Client Indicator – rebuilt using actual client history
  • Service & Region Segmentation – enabled hotspot detection

Key Insights

  • Execution risk is rising beneath the surface
    While on‑time delivery remains relatively stable, a growing share of active projects is under deadline pressure, indicating risk is building before failures become visible.
  • Delays are becoming structural, not temporary
    A large proportion of open work sits in long‑running aging buckets (180+ and 270+ days), pointing to persistent bottlenecks rather than short‑term slippage.
  • Backlog is growing faster than completion capacity
    Project intake consistently outpaces completion, leading to rising work‑in‑progress and increased execution pressure.
  • Risk and revenue exposure are highly concentrated
    Delivery risk is clustered in a small number of services and regions, meaning targeted intervention has far greater impact than broad fixes.
  • Delays weaken future client engagement
    Projects that remain overdue for extended periods show lower repeat engagement, reinforcing the link between delivery performance and long‑term client value.
  • Financial exposure clarifies priorities
    Framing delivery issues in terms of revenue at risk highlights urgency more effectively than project counts alone.

Overall insight: delivery outcomes can appear stable while execution pressure, backlog, and revenue risk quietly build underneath. Without early visibility and targeted intervention, these pressures compound and affect both operations and client retention.

Recommendations

  • Intervene earlier using active risk indicators
    Shift focus from completed outcomes to project age, deadline proximity, and delivery stage while recovery is still possible.
  • Target high‑impact service–region hotspots
    Concentrate corrective action where risk and revenue exposure are most concentrated.
  • Break structural delays
    Require mandatory review and escalation for projects exceeding aging thresholds (e.g., 180+ days).
  • Align intake with delivery capacity
    Adjust resourcing, prioritization, or intake pacing when backlog growth outpaces completion.
  • Treat delivery delays as a retention risk
    Proactively engage clients when projects fall behind to preserve trust and future engagement.
  • Use revenue‑at‑risk to guide decisions
    Frame delivery risk financially to focus leadership attention on the most consequential issues.

Bottom line: Delivery performance is holding today, but execution pressure is rising. Without early intervention in high‑impact areas, delivery risk will continue to compound and threaten long‑term sustainability.

What I Learned & Why It Matters

  • Backlog is a system signal, not just delayed work
    Aging patterns revealed structural bottlenecks, not isolated delivery issues.
  • Early signals matter more than final outcomes
    Stable on‑time delivery can hide growing execution pressure. Monitoring active work surfaces risk earlier.
  • Benchmarks provide context, not conclusions
    Using elapsed time and urgency avoided false alarms caused by rigid threshold comparisons.
  • Design should be driven by decisions, not visuals
    Removing metrics that didn’t support action improved clarity and decision focus.
  • Financial framing drives better decisions
    Expressing delivery issues as revenue at risk proved more effective than counts or percentages alone.

Final Takeaway

Delivery risk does not emerge at the point of failure, but much earlier. It builds through how work accumulates, ages, and competes for limited capacity. By reframing delivery monitoring around active execution signals rather than completed outcomes, risk becomes visible while intervention is still possible.

Operational data is most valuable when it supports prioritization. Translating delays into execution pressure and revenue exposure creates a clearer basis for deciding where to intervene, what to fix first, and which risks can no longer be ignored.
