Fintech Operations Workflow Analysis

A customer operations workflow showing how support tickets, PAYG account context, and troubleshooting reveal recurring issues and support faster resolution.

Freshdesk PAYG Platform Customer Operations Technical Troubleshooting Issue Pattern Analysis

Project Overview

This project presents a structured customer operations workflow in a PAYG fintech environment. It shows how ticket data, account context, and support patterns can improve response quality, escalation handling, and customer experience.

Customer Operations & Resolution Workflow

What happens from ticket review to learning.

1

Review & Understand Issue

Understand the request, history, and context.

What exactly is the customer reporting?
2

Verify Account & System Data

Check payment, account, device, and system records.

What does the system show?
3

Diagnose & Identify Root Cause

Compare customer reports with system evidence.

What is causing the issue?
4

Troubleshoot & Resolve

Guide the customer, test causes, and resolve within access level.

Can this be resolved now, or does it require escalation?
5

Communicate & Escalate

Provide updates, set expectations, and escalate with context.

What does the customer need to know? What does the next team need?
6

Document & Track Learning

Record actions and use recurring issues to improve support.

What insight can be captured to reduce future friction?

Customer Issues & How They Are Analyzed

Common issue areas reviewed during support.

Payment & Balance Issues

Verify mobile money transactions, payment states, and balance mismatches.

Account Access Problems

Check activation, access status, account updates, and payment response.

Device Lockout Issues

Review lock status and confirm whether payment should restore access.

Payment-to-System Sync

Track sync delays, missing updates, and system-side mismatches.

Repeated Follow-Up Cases

Identify unresolved or recurring issues and reduce repeat friction.

Risk / Fraud Signals

Detect conflicting information, ownership disputes, and high-risk cases.
